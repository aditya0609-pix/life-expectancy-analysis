Let's start

Is BMI related to life expectancy? If so, what is the trend in developing and developed nations and how is it related to life expectancy?

Explanation

  • Countries with high adult mortality levels have low on average BMI when compared to countries with less adult levels. The correlation between adult mortality levels and life expectancy is negative. Same results were observed in countries with less infant and children mortality levels. This suggests that people at the higher end of BMI range have higher life expectancy than those at the lower BMI range
  • Coming to the second part of the question, the average BMI in both developing and developed countries was increasing but it was significantly higher in developed countries when compared to developing countries.
