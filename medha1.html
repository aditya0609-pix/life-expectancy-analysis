Let's start

Do poor countries have less health care facilities for their people?

Explanation

  • GDP for both developed and developing countries was increasing over the years. On investigating the relationship between socioeconomic factors and life expectancy, we discovered that the number of infant and under-five deaths in countries with low income was significantly higher when compared to countries with high income. Infant and Under-Five deaths had a negative relationship with Life Expectancy. GDP had a positive relationship with percentage expenditure on healthcare services. This implies that wealthier countries spend more on healthcare. Or in other words, the people of these countries have access to resources that contribute to a long and healthy life like better healthcare facilities, clean water, sanitation facilities, etc. Infant and child deaths were chosen over adult deaths for this analysis because children who survive the first five years of their life are more likely to live to adulthood and old age. And this can be achieved only if infants, children, pregnant and nursing mothers are given proper nutritious food and good healthcare facilities. Therefore, the life expectancy in countries with high income and low infant and under-five deaths was found to be higher when compared to countries with low income and high infant and under-five deaths. Hence better the economic position of a country, lesser is the infant and child mortality levels and higher is the life expectancy.
